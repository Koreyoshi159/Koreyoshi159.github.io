Theory

Motivation is generally regarded as an important component for success, in school and at work. According to Kendra Cheery "What is Motivation?", there is two types of motivation: Extrinsic motivations and Intrinsic motivations. Extrinsic motivation comes from outside the person and often involve rewards, Intrinsic motivation comes from the person self and doing it for self-pleasure.


In the article of Marjetka Kastner “What are motivators and what is motivation?” is said motivation is a mental process that can lead people to their specific goal, there are also motivators that helps people to achieve motivation if they chose the right one.

According to Beata Souders “What is Motivation? A Psychologist Explains.”, there are five psychophysiological expressions of motivation:

  • Hormonal activity: When you stress or having a fight or flight reaction.
  • Cardiovascular activity: As response to an attractive incentive or a difficult/challenging task.
  • Ocular activity: Eye behavior like pupil size, eye blinks and eye movement.
  • Electrodermal activity: As response to threatening event.
  • Skeletal activity: facial expressions or bodily gestures etc.
